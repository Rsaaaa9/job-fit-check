## Rules / 规范

### ✅ DO

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Lead every bullet with an action verb | ✅ "Built a SQL dashboard..." not ❌ "Was responsible for building..." |
| Quantify everything possible | ✅ "50+ KOLs, ¥300K earned media, 15% reduction" |
| Mirror the JD's keywords | ✅ If JD says "user behavior analysis," your resume says "user behavior analysis" |
| Put the strongest content first in each section | ✅ Most relevant internship → first. Most impressive bullet → first. |
| 1 page for entry-level (unless 5+ relevant internships) | ✅ Edit ruthlessly. Every line earns its place. |
| PDF format for submission | ✅ Always PDF. Name it: `[YourName]_[CompanyName]_Resume.pdf` |

### ❌ DON'T

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Write "Responsible for," "Participated in," "Helped with" | These describe existence, not impact |
| Use personal pronouns ("I," "my," "me") | Resume convention — they know it's about you |
| Include a photo (outside CN/JP/KR/DE) | Opens bias risk; not expected in most markets |
| Rate your skills with bars or percentages | "Python: 80%" is meaningless and invites scrutiny |
| List every course you've taken | Cherry-pick 3-5 most relevant to the JD |
| Write more than 1 page (entry-level) | Recruiters spend 6 seconds. 2 pages = they won't read page 2. |
| Use a "Career Objective" section | This is 2005 thinking. Use a self-summary or skip it. |
| Include high school information | Unless you won a national competition, nobody cares. |

## Cheat Sheet: Action Verbs / 动词速查表

### By Situation

| Situation | Verbs |
|-----------|-------|
| You built something | Built, Developed, Launched, Created, Designed, Engineered, Shipped |
| You improved something | Optimized, Streamlined, Automated, Reduced, Increased, Accelerated |
| You analyzed something | Analyzed, Audited, Diagnosed, Evaluated, Forecasted, Modeled |
| You led something | Led, Coordinated, Directed, Facilitated, Organized, Spearheaded |
| You collaborated | Partnered, Liaised, Aligned, Negotiated, Bridged, Unified |
| You researched | Investigated, Surveyed, Benchmarked, Mapped, Profiled, Identified |
