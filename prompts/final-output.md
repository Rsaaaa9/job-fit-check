# ④ Final Output / 终稿输出

> **Purpose:** Assemble the optimized resume from Step ③ into a ready-to-submit document, write targeted Cover Letter talking points, and provide a channel-specific application strategy.

```
## FINAL OUTPUT FRAMEWORK

### ① Optimized Resume / 优化后简历全文

Assemble the rewritten content into a clean, ready-to-copy resume.

Formatting rules:
- Max 1 page for entry-level / new grad (unless 5+ internships, then 2 pages max)
- Section order optimized for THIS specific role:
  - Education goes FIRST if: target company is prestige-sensitive (consulting, investment banking, top-tier tech) OR candidate's school is a stronger signal than their experience
  - Experience goes FIRST if: candidate has 2+ relevant internships
  - Projects goes FIRST if: candidate has strong side projects but weak formal experience
- Consistent formatting: same bullet style, same date format, same indentation
- No personal pronouns ("I", "my", "me")
- No photos (unless applying in countries where photos are standard: CN, JP, KR, DE)
- File format recommendation: PDF unless explicitly asked for Word

Output the full resume in clean markdown that can be copied directly into
Google Docs / Word / LaTeX / Notion.

### ② Cover Letter Talking Points / 求职信要点

Do NOT write the full cover letter (it should be personalized by the candidate).
Instead, provide 3-4 talking points / story angles:

For each:
| Angle | Story to Tell | Why It Works for This JD |
|-------|---------------|--------------------------|
| [Label] | [2-3 sentence narrative] | [Connects to JD requirement X] |

Cover:
- Why this company (not "I admire your mission" — something specific and researched)
- Why this role (connect personal experience to the actual work)
- Why you're ready (bridge from past experience to day-1 contribution)
- What you'll bring (one unique insight or capability)

### ③ Application Strategy / 投递策略

Provide a channel-prioritized strategy:

| Priority | Channel | Why | Action Item |
|----------|---------|-----|-------------|
| 1 | [e.g., Employee Referral] | [Conversion rate / context] | [Specific action] |
| 2 | [e.g., Company Career Site] | [Reason] | [Specific action] |
| 3 | [e.g., Campus Recruiting] | [Reason] | [Specific action] |
| ... | ... | ... | ... |

Include:
- Best time to apply (day of week + time of day, based on recruiting patterns in this industry)
- Whether to attach a portfolio / work sample (and what kind)
- Follow-up timeline (when and how to follow up after applying)
- Any red flags in the JD that suggest this role might NOT be a good fit (be honest)

### ④ Pre-Interview Prep / 面试前准备

Based on the JD deconstruction (Step ①), provide:

- **Top 3 things to research** before the interview (company, team, industry)
- **3 questions the candidate should ASK** the interviewer (that demonstrate insight)
- **1-2 potential weaknesses** in this application and how to address them if brought up

---

## OUTPUT FORMAT

Output a complete, polished application package with all 4 sections.
The resume itself should be the centerpiece — a full, ready-to-use document.
```
