# Example 2: Digital Marketing (New Grad)

> **Scenario:** Marketing major with 1 agency internship applying for a brand marketing role at a consumer goods company (FMCG). The candidate has solid internship experience but the resume is written like a task list, not an impact story.

---

## Input Data

### Job Description

```
Brand Marketing Specialist — FMCG (Personal Care)

Join our 15-person marketing team driving growth for our flagship skincare
brand (¥500M+ annual revenue).

Requirements:
- Bachelor's degree or above, Marketing / Business / Communications preferred
- 2025/2026 graduate
- Strong data sensitivity — comfortable with sales data, market share analysis,
  and campaign ROI tracking
- Excellent copywriting and content creation skills
- Familiar with Xiaohongshu, Douyin, and WeChat ecosystem
- Bonus: experience with KOL/KOC collaboration, livestream e-commerce

Responsibilities:
- Support the brand manager on 360° marketing campaigns (online + offline)
- Manage the brand's Xiaohongshu and Douyin account content calendar
- Coordinate with agencies on KOL seeding campaigns and track ROI
- Analyze campaign performance data and produce post-mortem reports
- Monitor competitor activities and consumer trends in personal care
```

### Candidate Resume (condensed)

```
LI XIAO
📧 lixiao@email.com | 📱 139-xxxx-xxxx

EDUCATION
BA Marketing, South China University of Technology
Expected June 2026 | GPA: 3.7/4.0

INTERNSHIP
Marketing Intern, Ogilvy (July 2025 - Dec 2025)
- Assisted in the execution of marketing campaigns for a cosmetics client
- Helped organize KOL seeding for new product launch
- Participated in brainstorming sessions and took meeting minutes
- Managed sample inventory and shipping for seeding campaigns
- Translated campaign reports from English to Chinese

PROJECT
New Media Content Creation (Personal)
- Run a personal Xiaohongshu account about campus life with 800 followers
- Posted 30+ notes, highest single note impressions: 12,000

SKILLS
Microsoft Office, Canva, basic Photoshop, fluent English (CET-6: 580)

SELF-SUMMARY
Creative and hardworking marketing student with internship experience at a
top advertising agency. Passionate about brand marketing and social media.
Strong communication skills and attention to detail. Looking for a brand-side
marketing role to develop my skills in a fast-paced environment.
```

## Step ① Output: Job Deconstruction (summary)

### Hard Requirements

| Requirement | Strictness |
|-------------|-----------|
| Bachelor's degree | 🔴 Must-have |
| Marketing/Business/Communications preferred | 🟡 Preferred (✅ SATISFIED) |
| 2025/2026 graduate | 🔴 Must-have (✅ SATISFIED) |
| Data sensitivity — sales data, market share, ROI | 🟡 Preferred (⚠️ NOT DEMONSTRATED in resume) |
| Copywriting & content creation | 🔴 Must-have |
| Xiaohongshu, Douyin, WeChat familiarity | 🔴 Must-have |
| KOL/KOC collaboration (bonus) | 🟢 Bonus (⚠️ HAS EXPERIENCE but didn't frame it well) |

### Hidden Requirements

| Hidden Need | Why | How to Signal |
|-------------|-----|---------------|
| **Business acumen** | FMCG brand marketing is a P&L-driven function. You're not "being creative" — you're spending budget to drive revenue. | Every campaign bullet must connect creative execution to business outcome. |
| **Agency management skills** | "Coordinate with agencies" means you'll manage external partners who cost ¥100K+/month. Can you push them without being pushed? | Show instances of managing third parties (even sample logistics counts). |
| **Cross-functional coordination** | Brand marketing sits between sales, supply chain, finance, and external agencies. You'll spend 40% of time aligning stakeholders. | Add "coordinated with X departments" framing to every experience. |
| **Consumer of personal care content** | If you're applying to market skincare, you should already be a heavy consumer of skincare content. It shows genuine interest. | Mention personal engagement with the category in self-summary or interview. |

### Core Competencies

| Category | Day 1 | Month 1 | Not Required |
|----------|-------|---------|--------------|
| Hard Skills | Copywriting (social media native), Canva/PS basics | Campaign ROI calculation, media budget tracking, campaign calendar management | Advanced Excel modeling |
| Platforms | Xiaohongshu, Douyin (as a user) | Xiaohongshu/Douyin professional backend, KOL platform tools | Programmatic ad buying |
| Soft Skills | Clear written communication, detail orientation | Agency briefing, internal presentation, timeline management | Crisis PR handling |

### Interview Attack Surface (top 5)

| # | Question | What They're Testing |
|---|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | "Walk me through a campaign you worked on — what was YOUR specific contribution, and what was the result?" | Ability to separate personal contribution from team outcome. Data fluency. |
| 2 | "A KOL you seeded for ¥50K only generated 200 clicks. What went wrong? What would you do differently?" | ROI orientation, analytical thinking, accountability. |
| 3 | "If we're launching a new moisturizer for Gen Z, which platform and content strategy would you lead with?" | Platform intuition, target audience understanding, strategic thinking. |
| 4 | "What's a personal care brand you think is doing great on Xiaohongshu right now? Why?" | Category passion, competitive awareness, marketing taste. |
| 5 | "How would you brief an agency for a KOL campaign with a ¥200K budget?" | Agency management, structured communication, budget sense. |

## Step ② Output: Resume Diagnosis (summary)

### Matches

| Resume Line | Maps To | Strength |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| BA Marketing at SCUT, GPA 3.7 | Degree requirement | ⭐⭐⭐ |
| Ogilvy internship (top agency, cosmetics client) | Industry relevance + prestige signal | ⭐⭐⭐ |
| KOL seeding involvement | KOL/KOC bonus requirement | ⭐⭐ (needs stronger framing) |
| Personal Xiaohongshu account (800 followers, 12K impressions) | Platform familiarity | ⭐⭐ (underutilized — this is a STRONG signal for a new grad!) |
| English: CET-6 580 | International brand communication (many FMCG brands have global teams) | ⭐⭐ (should be framed as a skill, not just a test score) |

### Redundancies

| Line | Why Redundant | Action |
|------|-------------|--------|
| "Participated in brainstorming sessions and took meeting minutes" | Participation is not impact. Anyone can take notes. | 🗑️ DELETE completely |
| "Creative and hardworking" in self-summary | Every candidate says this | 🗑️ DELETE |
| "Passionate about brand marketing" in self-summary | Show, don't tell. The rest of the resume should prove this. | 🗑️ DELETE |
| "Strong communication skills and attention to detail" | Generic + used in 90% of self-summaries | 🗑️ DELETE |
| "Translated campaign reports from English to Chinese" | Unless applying for a translation-heavy role, this is minor | ✂️ CONDENSE to a skills addendum |
| "basic Photoshop" in skills | "Basic" undersells you | 🔄 REPURPOSE: "Photoshop (social media asset creation)" |

### Gaps

| JD Requirement | Status | Severity | Mitigation |
|---------------|--------|----------|------------|
| "Data sensitivity — sales data, ROI tracking" | ❌ COMPLETELY MISSING | 🔴 DEALBREAKER | This is the #1 gap. The candidate's resume has ZERO numbers. Reframe every bullet with data. If they don't have exact data, use directional numbers they can defend. |
| "Manage content calendar" | ⚠️ HINTED AT (ran personal account) | 🟡 SIGNIFICANT | Add platform-specific content strategy bullets to the personal Xiaohongshu project. Show they didn't just post randomly — they had a strategy. |
| "Campaign post-mortem reports" | ⚠️ HINTED AT (translated reports) | 🟡 SIGNIFICANT | Reframe: "translated reports" → "analyzed campaign performance data for post-mortem reports." It's true and it's what they need to see. |
| "Competitor monitoring" | ❌ COMPLETELY MISSING | 🟢 MINOR | Add a line about tracking competitor campaigns while at Ogilvy (accurate — agency people naturally track competitors). |

### Risks

| Resume Line | What It Signals | Risk | Fix |
|-------------|-----------------|------|-----|
| All Ogilvy bullets are passive ("Assisted," "Helped," "Participated") | "This person was a warm body in the room, not a contributor" | 🔴 HIGH | Rewrite every bullet to put the candidate in the driver's seat. Active verbs. |
| "Managed sample inventory and shipping" | "Intern doing intern-level logistics, not marketing" | 🟡 MEDIUM | Delete or reframe as "managed ¥XXK product seeding logistics, ensuring 98% on-time delivery for 50+ KOLs" |
| Self-summary is entirely generic | "I don't know what makes me different from 200 other applicants" | 🟡 MEDIUM | Rewrite with specific, quantifiable hooks |
| 800 followers on Xiaohongshu | Some might think "that's not impressive" | 🟢 LOW | Reframe: don't mention follower count. Focus on content strategy, engagement rate, and platform understanding. 12K impressions on a single note from an 800-follower account is actually a decent engagement rate (15x). |

### Pre-Interview Prep

**Top 3 things to research:**
1. Go to a Watsons / Sephora and spend 30 minutes in the skincare aisle. Take photos of the brand's shelf placement, price position vs. competitors, and promotional materials.
2. Follow the brand's official Xiaohongshu and Douyin accounts. Document their last 10 posts — what's the content mix? Which posts perform best? What's missing?
3. Search the brand name + "review" on Xiaohongshu. Read 20 user reviews. Identify 3 recurring consumer pain points and 3 things consumers love.

**Questions to ASK the interviewer:**
1. "How does the brand team measure marketing success — is it more brand health metrics (awareness, consideration) or performance metrics (ROI, conversion)?" → Shows understanding of the brand vs. performance marketing tension.
2. "What's the relationship like between the brand team and the sales team? How do you collaborate on trade marketing?" → FMCG-specific question that demonstrates industry knowledge.
3. "I noticed your brand's recent Douyin content is mostly product demonstration — have you experimented with storytelling or behind-the-scenes formats?" → Shows you did your homework and came with ideas.

**Application weaknesses & how to address them:**
1. **No brand-side experience** → "Agency experience taught me to be execution-focused and deadline-driven, which I think is a better foundation than starting on the brand side. I know how agencies think, so I can be a better client."
2. **Still developing data analysis skills** → "I'm comfortable with campaign-level metrics and can build tracking dashboards. I'm currently learning more advanced Excel and SQL to go deeper into consumer data analysis."
